Japchae

Description

Japchae is a savory and slightly sweet dish of stir-fried glass noodles and vegetables that is popular in Korean cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 250g Korean sweet potato starch noodles
  • 100g ribe eye fillet, cut into strips
  • 1 carrot
  • 110g baby spinach
  • 1/4 red bell pepper
  • 1/2 yellow onion
  • 100g shiitake mushrrom
  • 1/4 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1 tsp minced garlic
  • 2 tsp toasted sesame oil
  • 5 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp rice wine
  • 3/8 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
  • 1 Tbsp honey
  • 1 Tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 Tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 1 Tbsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 1 extra large egg

Steps

  1. Place the beef strips into a medium bowl. Add 1 Tbsp soy sauce, the rice wine, half of the minced garlic, 1/4 tsp ground black pepper and 1 tsp toasted sesame oil. Gently mix together and set aside, cover and set aside whilst working on the other ingredients.
  2. Rinse and peel the carrot and then cut julienne style.
  3. Rinse and cut the pepper julienne style
  4. Peel the onion and thinly slice.
  5. Clean the shiitake mushrooms, remove the stems and then thinly slice.
  6. Mix 4 Tbsp soy sauce, the honey, brown sugar, 1 Tbsp toasted sesame oil and 1/8 tsp ground black pepper into a bowl.
  7. Put the sliced mushroom into a bowl and then add 1 Tbsp of the above mixture. Mix the two well and then set aside.
  8. Blanch the spinach in rolling boiling water (5-10 seconds). Drain the water and quickly cool it down by running it under cold tap water. Squeeze the spinach to remove any excess water and put it into a mixing bowl.
  9. Add 1/4 tsp fine sea salt, 1/2 tsp minced garlic and 1 tsp toasted sesame oil to the spinach and pix gentley. Put into a large mixing bowl where we will be adding the rest of the prepared ingredients later.
  10. Boil some water in a large pot. Once the water is boiling, add the noodles and boil them for 6-7 minutes. Drain the water. Rinse in cold water to cool down and let the water drain for 1-2 minutes.
  11. Cut the noodles with a pair of kitchen scissors a couple of times (between 15-20cm). Move the noodles into a mixing bowl and add the rest of the mixture we used to marinate the mushrooms. Mix well and set aside.
  12. Separate the yolk and the egg white. Then beat both with a fork.
  13. Add the egg white to a heated pan and cook both sides on low heat. Transfer to a clean cutting board.
  14. Add the yolk to a heated pan and cook both sides on low heat. Transfer to a clean cutting board.
  15. Add the onions to the pan and cook until they soften, then add to the mixing bowl with the spinach.
  16. Add the carrots to the pan and cook until they soften, then add to the mixing bowl with the spinach.
  17. Add the peppers to the pan and cook until they soften, then add to the mixing bowl with the spinach.
  18. Add the mushrooms and sauce to the pan and cook until they are cooked, then add to the mixing bowl with the spinach.
  19. Add the marinated meat to the pan and cook until they are cooked, then add to the mixing bowl with the spinach.
  20. Add the noodles to the pan and cook until they are coated in the marinade, then add to the mixing bowl with the spinach.
  21. Thinly slice the egg white and egg yolk like match sticks. Add them to the mixing bowl as above.
  22. To the mixing bowl add 1 Tbsp toasted sesame seeds and 1 Tbsp toasted sesame oil. Gently mix them around with your hands.
  23. Serve and enjoy.
